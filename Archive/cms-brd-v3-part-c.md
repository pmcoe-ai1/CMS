### Part C: Visitor Experience

The requirements in this part define the experience for visitors to the published websites and for external AI agents interacting with those websites. The "Traditional Interface" and "AI Interface" columns in sections 5.10 and 5.12 describe how visitors accomplish tasks through conventional browsing vs through the visitor-facing AI chat widget. The columns in section 5.11 describe how the tenant's administrator configures and monitors AI-to-AI interoperability through the traditional admin interface vs through the admin AI chat panel. All visitor experience features are tenant-scoped — they operate on the publishing tenant's content and configuration only (per 5.9.11).

### 5.10 Visitor Interaction

| ID | Requirement | Traditional Interface (visitor) | AI Interface (visitor) |
|----|-------------|--------------------------------|----------------------|
| 5.10.1 | Every published website must provide visitors with two co-equal interaction methods available on all pages:<br>1) Traditional navigation — menus, links, buttons, forms, search, pagination, and all standard web interaction patterns.<br>2) An AI conversational interface — a chat widget accessible on desktop and mobile devices without disrupting the traditional browsing experience.<br>Both methods must be available simultaneously. Neither is subordinate to the other. | The website renders with all traditional navigation elements fully functional. The chat widget appears as a non-intrusive floating icon (typically bottom-right corner) that does not obscure or displace page content. Visitors who ignore the widget browse the site exactly as they would without it. | Visitor clicks the chat widget to open a conversation panel. The panel overlays or docks alongside the page content. Visitor types or speaks a question and receives a response. The widget can be minimised or dismissed at any time without affecting the traditional interface beneath it. |
| 5.10.2 | Visitors must be able to perform all website interactions through either interface: browsing pages, searching content, reading information, submitting forms, requesting quotes, making purchases, booking appointments, asking questions, and any other action available on the website. No functionality may be exclusive to one interface. | Visitor navigates using menus, clicks links, submits forms, uses search bars, and completes transactions through dedicated pages. Each action requires navigating to the relevant page and completing the interaction manually. | Visitor says "show me your certification courses" and the AI displays relevant course information in the chat. Visitor says "enrol me in the March course" and the AI guides them through the enrolment workflow conversationally — without requiring navigation to separate pages. |
| 5.10.3 | The website must support natural language queries from visitors and respond with accurate information drawn exclusively from the publishing tenant's content. | Visitor uses the site search bar to find information, then reads the relevant page. Results depend on the visitor formulating an effective search query using keywords. | Visitor asks "What courses do you have starting next month in Sydney?" and the AI responds with an accurate, real-time answer sourced from the website's live course listings — including dates, prices, and availability. |
| 5.10.4 | The website must support multi-step workflows — such as enrolment, checkout, form completion, appointment booking, or account creation — completable through either interface without requiring the visitor to switch between them. | Visitor follows a multi-page flow: selects a product or service, navigates to a checkout page, fills in a form, completes payment. Each step requires a page load and manual input. | Visitor says "I want to book a consultation" and the AI collects the required information step by step within the conversation: date preference, time, contact details, confirmation. The entire workflow completes within the chat panel. |
| 5.10.5 | The website must support voice input and voice output for visitors who prefer spoken interaction, compatible with device-native speech capabilities and voice assistants (Siri, Google Assistant, Alexa). | Not applicable to traditional interface — voice interaction is delivered through the AI chat widget. The traditional interface uses standard keyboard and mouse/touch input. | Visitor taps a microphone icon in the chat widget and speaks their question. The AI responds with both text and synthesised speech output. On devices with voice assistant integration, the visitor can invoke the website's AI through their device assistant. |
| 5.10.6 | The website must support multimodal interaction, allowing visitors to combine text, voice, and visual elements (such as uploading images, sharing screenshots, or clicking on inline product cards and interactive elements within the chat). | Visitor interacts with page elements visually — clicking images, buttons, and links. File uploads happen through standard form fields on specific pages. | Visitor drags an image into the chat panel or uses the attachment button and says "do you have anything similar to this?" The AI analyses the image and responds with relevant products or content. Visitor clicks on a product card displayed within the chat to view details or add to cart. |
| 5.10.7 | The website must personalise visitor interactions based on context, including geographic location, device type, browsing history within the session, referral source, and any preferences the visitor has expressed. | The website displays location-based content or personalised recommendations through traditional personalisation features (cookies, geo-IP, account preferences). Personalisation is applied to page content and layout. | Visitor in Sydney asks "what's available near me?" and the AI responds with location-relevant results. The AI remembers within the session that the visitor expressed interest in advanced courses and prioritises those in subsequent recommendations. |
| 5.10.8 | The website must provide real-time information to visitors, including current pricing, availability, schedules, stock levels, and any dynamic content that changes over time, drawn directly from the website's live data. | Visitor navigates to the relevant page to check current pricing or availability. Information accuracy depends on the page being up to date and the visitor refreshing the page. | Visitor asks "is the March workshop still available?" and the AI checks live data and responds with current availability and pricing — reflecting any changes made since the page was last loaded. |
| 5.10.9 | The website must support presenting rich content to visitors including images, product cards, comparison tables, videos, maps, calendar views, and interactive elements — through both the page layout and within the AI conversation. | Visitor views rich content by navigating to the relevant page where images, videos, tables, and interactive elements are rendered as part of the page layout. | Visitor asks "compare your two certification programs" and the AI displays a comparison table within the chat. Visitor asks "show me your office location" and the AI displays an embedded map. Product cards within the chat include images, pricing, and action buttons. |
| 5.10.10 | The tenant's administrator must be able to configure the visitor interaction experience: the AI interface's personality, tone, allowed topics, restricted information, escalation rules, and business-specific knowledge. | Tenant Administrator opens the visitor AI configuration panel in the admin interface. Configures personality (formal/casual), tone, restricted topics, escalation triggers, and uploads business-specific knowledge documents. | Tenant Administrator says "set the visitor AI tone to professional and friendly" and the system updates the configuration. Administrator says "restrict the visitor AI from discussing competitor products" and the system adds the guardrail. Administrator says "upload this FAQ document as knowledge for the visitor AI" and the system ingests it. |
| 5.10.11 | The website must support seamless handoff from the AI interface to human support when the visitor requests it or when the conversation exceeds the AI's capability, with full conversation context transferred to the human agent. | Visitor clicks a "Contact Us" or "Live Chat" button to reach human support. A new conversation starts with a human agent. No context from prior browsing or AI conversation is carried over unless specifically implemented. | Visitor says "I'd like to speak to a person" or the AI detects it cannot adequately answer. The AI says "Let me connect you with our team" and transfers the full conversation transcript to a human agent. The human agent sees everything the visitor discussed and continues without the visitor needing to repeat themselves. |
| 5.10.12 | The website must maintain visitor interaction continuity within a session and, with the visitor's consent, across sessions to provide personalisation and context retention. | Session continuity in traditional browsing relies on cookies, account login state, and browser history. Returning visitors see personalised content if they are logged in or have accepted cookies. | Visitor returns to the website and the AI says "Welcome back — last time you were looking at our advanced certification. Would you like to continue?" (with consent). Within a single session, the AI remembers all prior questions and does not ask for information the visitor has already provided. |

### 5.11 AI-to-AI Interoperability

All AI-to-AI protocol endpoints are tenant-scoped (per 5.9.11). External AI agents interact with a specific tenant's website, not the platform as a whole. The "Traditional Interface" and "AI Interface" columns in this section describe how the tenant's administrator configures and monitors these endpoints.

| ID | Requirement | Traditional Interface | AI Interface |
|----|-------------|----------------------|-------------|
| 5.11.1 | Each tenant's website must expose a Model Context Protocol (MCP) server endpoint that allows external AI agents (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, Perplexity, and others) to programmatically discover, query, and interact with the tenant's published content, products, services, and data in a structured, standardised way. | Tenant Administrator opens the AI-to-AI interoperability panel in admin settings. The MCP server endpoint URL is displayed with its status (active/inactive). Administrator can enable or disable the endpoint. | Administrator says "show me our MCP server status" and the system displays the endpoint URL and its current state. Administrator says "enable the MCP server" and the system activates it. |
| 5.11.2 | Each tenant's website must support the Agent-to-Agent (A2A) protocol (Google/Linux Foundation open standard), publishing an Agent Card in JSON format that advertises the website's capabilities, supported interactions, authentication requirements, and service endpoints — enabling external AI agents to discover and delegate tasks to the website's AI. | Administrator opens the A2A configuration panel. Reviews the auto-generated Agent Card JSON. Edits capability descriptions, supported interactions, and authentication settings. Publishes or unpublishes the Agent Card. | Administrator says "show me our A2A Agent Card" and the system displays the JSON. Administrator says "update the Agent Card to advertise our booking capability" and the system modifies and republishes it. |
| 5.11.3 | Each tenant's website must support the Agent Communication Protocol (ACP) for standardised RESTful messaging between the website's AI and external agents, supporting both synchronous and asynchronous interactions, task delegation, and multi-agent coordination. | Administrator opens the ACP configuration panel. Reviews active endpoints, message formats, and supported interaction modes. Configures synchronous/asynchronous settings. | Administrator says "enable asynchronous messaging on our ACP endpoint" and the system applies the configuration. Administrator says "show me recent ACP interactions" and the system displays the interaction log. |
| 5.11.4 | For e-commerce or transactional websites, the system must support the Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) and Agentic Commerce Protocol, enabling external AI agents to discover products, create checkout sessions, process payments, and complete purchases on behalf of users through standardised commerce primitives. | Administrator opens the commerce protocol panel. Enables UCP and configures which products and services are available to external agents. Sets checkout and payment processing rules. | Administrator says "enable UCP for our product catalogue" and the system activates it. Administrator says "restrict external agents to purchases under $500" and the system applies the limit. |
| 5.11.5 | For e-commerce or transactional websites, the system must support the Agent Payments Protocol (AP2) for secure, cryptographically signed payment authorisation between AI agents, payment processors (Stripe, PayPal, Visa, Mastercard, American Express), and the website's commerce infrastructure. | Administrator opens the AP2 configuration panel. Connects payment processors, configures authorisation rules, and sets transaction limits. Reviews signed payment mandates. | Administrator says "connect Stripe to our AP2 endpoint" and the system opens the integration setup. Administrator says "require human approval for agent payments over $200" and the system applies the rule. |
| 5.11.6 | The website's MCP server must expose tools that allow external AI agents to search the product/service catalogue, check availability and pricing, retrieve content, submit inquiries, initiate bookings or enrolments, and perform any visitor-facing interaction available through the website. | Administrator opens the MCP tools panel. Reviews the auto-generated tool list with descriptions. Enables or disables individual tools. Edits tool descriptions and parameter schemas. | Administrator says "show me all MCP tools we expose" and the system lists them. Administrator says "disable the booking tool from external agents" and the system removes it from the MCP server. Administrator says "what tools are external agents using most?" and the system shows usage statistics. |
| 5.11.7 | All AI-to-AI interactions must be secured with HTTPS/TLS, support authentication schemes (OAuth 2.1, OpenID Connect, API keys, or mutual TLS), and enforce rate limiting, scope restrictions, and access controls to prevent unauthorised access or abuse. | System-level enforcement — all endpoints are served over HTTPS with authentication required. Administrator opens the security settings for AI-to-AI endpoints to configure authentication method, rate limits, and scope restrictions per protocol. | Administrator says "set the MCP rate limit to 100 requests per minute" and the system applies it. Administrator says "require OAuth 2.1 for all external agent connections" and the system enforces the policy. |
| 5.11.8 | The tenant must maintain an audit trail of all AI-to-AI interactions, logging which external agents accessed what data, when, and what actions were taken, accessible to the tenant's administrator. | Administrator opens the AI-to-AI audit log panel. Log entries show timestamp, agent identity, protocol used, tool invoked, data accessed, and action taken. Filterable by agent, protocol, date range, and action type. | Administrator says "show me all external agent interactions this week" and the system opens the filtered audit log. Administrator says "which AI agents accessed our product catalogue today?" and the system lists them with details. |
| 5.11.9 | The system must automatically keep MCP server tools, A2A Agent Cards, and ACP endpoints synchronised with the website's content, so that when pages, products, pricing, or availability change, external AI agents always receive current information. | System-level — synchronisation runs automatically when content changes. A status indicator in the interoperability panel shows the last sync time and any sync errors. | System-level — synchronisation is automatic. Administrator says "when was the MCP server last synced?" and the system reports the timestamp. Administrator says "force a sync now" and the system refreshes all endpoints. |
| 5.11.10 | The tenant's administrator must be able to configure which content, data, and actions are exposed to external AI agents, including the ability to restrict sensitive information, limit transaction types, and control which agent platforms are granted access. | Administrator opens the agent access control panel. A checklist shows content areas, tools, and actions with enable/disable toggles. An agent allowlist/denylist panel lets the administrator specify which AI platforms can connect. | Administrator says "hide our pricing from external AI agents" and the system removes pricing data from all protocol endpoints. Administrator says "only allow ChatGPT and Claude to connect to our MCP server" and the system applies the allowlist. Administrator says "block all agent access to the enrolment tool" and the system disables it. |

### 5.12 Traditional Navigation Preservation

| ID | Requirement | Traditional Interface (visitor) | AI Interface (visitor) |
|----|-------------|--------------------------------|----------------------|
| 5.12.1 | All traditional navigation elements (menus, links, buttons, forms, search, pagination, breadcrumbs) must remain fully functional alongside the AI interface. The website design must not degrade or replace the traditional browsing experience for visitors who prefer it. | Visitor browses the website using menus, clicks links, submits forms, uses the search bar, follows breadcrumbs, and paginates through lists — all functioning identically to a website without an AI chat widget. The AI widget's presence does not alter the layout, functionality, or accessibility of any traditional navigation element. | The AI chat widget is available as an alternative interaction method. Visitors who use the AI widget still have full access to all traditional navigation elements on the page behind the chat panel. The AI may reference traditional navigation elements in its responses (e.g., "You can also find this on our Courses page in the main menu"). |
| 5.12.2 | The website must be fully usable with the AI interface disabled or dismissed, ensuring accessibility for visitors who cannot or choose not to use AI interaction. | If the AI widget is dismissed, closed, or disabled by the tenant administrator, the website continues to function completely — every page, form, link, menu, search, and interactive element works without the AI. No functionality depends exclusively on the AI interface. | Not applicable — this requirement ensures the website functions without the AI interface. There is no AI interface column because the requirement specifically addresses the scenario where the AI is absent. |
| 5.12.3 | The AI interface and traditional navigation must share the same underlying data and content, ensuring consistency — a visitor who browses a page and a visitor who asks the AI about the same content must receive identical, accurate information. | Visitor navigates to a product page and sees the current price, description, and availability displayed on the page. | Visitor asks the AI "what's the price of [product]?" and receives the exact same price, description, and availability shown on the product page. If the page content is updated, the AI's responses update immediately — there is no lag or inconsistency between the two interfaces. |
